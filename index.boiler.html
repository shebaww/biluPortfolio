The Introduction

Hi, I'm Naomi!

[Photo 2: About]

I'm your RA for this upcoming year, and I'm excited to meet you! I'm from Addis Ababa, Ethiopia — home and family mean everything to me, so I understand how difficult it is to be so far away from home.

As a member of the African Student Association and a Senator in the Student Government Association, I'm actively involved on campus. I'm a Political Science major pursuing a minor in Legal Studies and a certificate in African Sustainability and Development.

In my free time, I love reading, painting, spending time with friends and family, and baking. If you're lucky, you might get to try one of my sweet treats!

Whether it's late-night chats or conflict mediations, I can't wait to make you feel like Penn State is a home away from home.

The Role

What is an RA?

"A Resident Assistant is a trained peer tasked with fostering an inclusive community and helping students adjust to college life."

🌉

Bridge Builder

Connecting students to the institution, resources, and to each other.

🤝

Peer First

We're students too. we understand your perspective and respond accordingly.

🏠

Community Creator

Making the residence hall feel like a genuine home away from home.

An RA serves as a bridge between students and the institution. My role extends far beyond enforcing rules—it's about creating a safe and welcoming environment where everyone can thrive.

The Core

My Strengths

Based on CliftonStrengths — how I show up for my community.

Strategic

I naturally look for patterns and think several steps ahead. I help residents see a path forward with multiple options so they can choose what feels right.

Positivity

I encourage people and keep morale up during stressful periods, while remaining mindful that sometimes people just need space to feel first.

Futuristic

I stay motivated by the bigger picture, reminding residents that difficult moments are temporary and that progress is always happening.

Consistency

I believe in fairness and transparency, holding the same standards for every resident to create a sense of trust and predictability.

Focus

I follow through on commitments and stay on track with goals, ensuring that my residents always have a reliable point of contact.

The Toolkit

Transferable Skills

Time Management

01

I balance a full class schedule with duty nights, program planning, and personal life. Living where I work requires clear systems like color-coded calendars and disciplined prioritization.

Cultural Awareness

02

Supporting residents from diverse backgrounds has taught me to approach every interaction with an open mind, adapting my style to meet people where they are.

Conflict Mediation

03

I help residents find resolutions during disputes by staying calm, listening carefully, and using de-escalation frameworks—a skill invaluable in any workplace.

Intentional Community Building

04

Designing experiences based on resident needs requires curiosity and consistency. Communities don't build themselves; they need dedicated leadership.

Marketing Mindset

05

I use my social media experience to capture attention and frame events so they feel worth attending, thinking carefully about language and visuals that connect.

The RA role is where leadership meets empathy. It's a professional development experience that builds skills applicable across nearly every career field.
