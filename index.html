The Energy.

Hi! I'm Naomi Teklemariam (she/her), your RA for this upcoming year, and I'm excited to meet you. I'm from Addis Ababa, Ethiopia — home and family mean everything to me, so I understand how difficult it is to be so far away from home. Whenever you're feeling homesick, I'm always here for you.

As a member of the African Student Association and a Senator in the Student Government Association, I'm actively involved on campus. I'm a Political Science major pursuing a minor in Legal Studies and a certificate in African Sustainability and Development.

You can think of me as Mike Ross, just a bit more forgetful! Some of my favourite pastimes include reading, painting, and baking. If you're lucky, you might get to try one of my sweet treats.

I'm really looking forward to getting to know every one of you. Whether it's through one-on-one meetings or late-night chats, I can't wait to make you feel like Penn State is home away from home.

What is an RA?

"A trained peer and residence life staff member tasked with fostering an inclusive community and helping students adjust to college life."

🌉

Bridge Builder

Connecting students to resources, support systems, and the institution.

🤝

Peer First

I'm a student too. I understand your perspective and respond accordingly.

🏠

Community Creator

Making the residence hall feel like a genuine home away from home.

As your RA, my role extends far beyond enforcing rules and filing reports. The RA position is fundamental to creating a safe and welcoming environment. It means managing residential responsibilities alongside academic ones while playing an essential part in helping both students and the institution thrive.

The Blueprint.

Learning about Student Development Theories has fundamentally shaped the way I approach my role. These frameworks help me guide residents toward personal growth.

Alexander Astin

Involvement Theory

Time and energy invested directly impacts development. I tailor programs to residents' actual interests—not just what's convenient—to create experiences they genuinely want to return to.

Baxter Magolda

Self-Authorship Theory

The process of defining one's own identity. I guide residents through self-discovery, recommending resources like CAPS and scheduling one-on-ones to assess their growth.

Vincent Tinto

Student Retention Theory

Students persist when academically and socially integrated. By organizing Community Builder events, I apply this theory to ensure every student feels they belong.

My Aura.

Based on CliftonStrengths — how I show up for the floor.

Strategic

I naturally look for patterns and think steps ahead. When a resident feels stuck, I help them see several paths forward so they can choose what feels right for them.

Positivity

I encourage people and keep morale up, especially during stress like midterms. I'm mindful that sometimes people need space to feel first, and I will meet them there.

Futuristic

Motivated by the bigger picture, I remind residents that difficult moments are temporary. I am actively working on sitting with the process rather than rushing.

Consistency

I believe in fairness and transparency. I hold the same standards for every resident to create a sense of trust and predictability on the floor.

Focus

Focus helps me follow through on what I start and meet deadlines. I am learning to manage unexpected shifts with more flexibility while staying on track.

The Toolkit.

01

Time Management

Balancing a full class schedule with duty nights and program planning. Unlike most jobs, RAs live where they work. I create clear systems like color-coded calendars to protect my priorities and stay organized.

02

Cultural Awareness

Supporting residents from diverse backgrounds has taught me to approach every interaction with an open mind. I meet residents where they are, adapting my style to ensure everyone feels seen and supported.

03

Conflict Mediation

Staying calm and listening carefully during disputes. The ability to navigate conflict professionally and guide parties toward workable solutions is invaluable in any future career field.

04

Intentional Community Building

Designing experiences based on resident needs requires curiosity and consistency. Communities don't build themselves; they need someone willing to do the work and adapt as things change.

05

Marketing Mindset

I approach program attendance like a marketer—thinking about audience, language, and visuals. I create flyers and messaging that make residents stop scrolling and want to show up.

The Zombie Run Experience

When I participated in Zombie Run, I was initially anxious about meeting new people in an unfamiliar setting. But the program's structure—built around collaboration—made it easy to connect naturally. I left feeling more confident and connected. As an RA, I will design experiences that make residents feel they genuinely belong.

The Vision.

"At the end of an academic year, I want my residents to leave feeling more confident in who they are."

I want them to feel like they had someone in their corner: someone that checked in when things got hard and celebrated them when things went well. Whether it's meaningful connection, resource awareness, or self-advocacy—that is the community I am committed to building.
